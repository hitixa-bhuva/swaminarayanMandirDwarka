Bet Dwarka, also known as Shankhodhar, is said to have been the residence of Lord Krishna during his ruling years at Dwarka. It derived its name from the word 'bet' which translates to gift and is believed that Lord Krishna received it from his friend Sudama. In the ancient epic, Mahabharata, Bet Dwarka is known as Antardvipa to which people of the Yadava clan needed to travel by boat.

From Dwarka: 30 km via Okha Road

From Jamnagar: 137 km via NH947A

From Rajkot: 217 km via NH27 and NH947A

Take a ferry from Okha to reach Bet Dwarka island

Nearest Station: Okha Railway Station (5 km)

Major Connection: Dwarka Railway Station (35 km)

Express Trains: Okha Express, Saurashtra Express

Take local transport from station to Okha port, then ferry to island

Nearest Airport: Jamnagar Airport (137 km)

Major Airport: Rajkot Airport (217 km)

International: Ahmedabad Airport (450 km)
